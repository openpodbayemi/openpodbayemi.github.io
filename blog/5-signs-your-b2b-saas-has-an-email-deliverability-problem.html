5 signs your B2B SaaS has an email deliverability problem

If you run a B2B SaaS, email is one of your most important distribution channels. Onboarding sequences, product updates, billing reminders, and lifecycle campaigns all depend on one thing: actually landing in your customer’s inbox. The problem is that deliverability issues rarely announce themselves. Unless you’re looking closely at the right signals, you can quietly bleed signups and revenue for months. Here are five signs your SaaS might have an email deliverability problem. ## 1. Customers say they ‘never got the email’ If support tickets regularly include lines like ‘I didn’t receive the confirmation email’ or ‘your reset link never arrived,’ treat that as a red flag. One or two cases might be user error; a pattern usually means spam filtering, blocked domains, or poor inbox placement. ## 2. Open rates suddenly drop without a clear reason Every list changes over time, but a sharp, sustained drop in open rates (without a big change in audience, subject lines, or sending frequency) often points to deliverability trouble. ISPs may be throttling or bulk-foldering your messages after seeing low engagement or spam complaints. ## 3. Different providers behave very differently Look at performance by domain. If Gmail users open at 35% but Outlook or corporate domains are flatlining, there may be configuration issues (SPF/DKIM/DMARC), IP reputation problems, or content that gets flagged in more conservative environments. ## 4. You see more bounces and spam complaints High hard-bounce rates are a sign of an unhealthy list or aggressive acquisition tactics. Spam complaints (even at low absolute numbers) are a strong negative signal to mailbox providers. Both can push more of your future messages into spam or Promotions, even for engaged users. ## 5. Key lifecycle emails underperform your marketing emails If transactional or onboarding emails — things like account confirmation, password reset, or trial activation — perform worse than your marketing broadcasts, something is off.
